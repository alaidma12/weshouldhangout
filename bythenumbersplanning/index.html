By the numbers

These aren't rare patterns. The research is consistent across cultures, ages, and contexts.

30%
Only 30% of people finished a task on the day they predicted — even when the task was familiar.
The other 70% were confident, wrong, and surprised. That's the Planning Fallacy at work.
Source: Buehler, R., Griffin, D., & Ross, M. (1994). Exploring the "planning fallacy": Why people underestimate their task completion times. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 67, 366–381.
80%
About 80% of people display optimism bias — across different ages, genders, and cultures worldwide.
Most of them have no idea they have it. Which, arguably, is the whole point.
Source: Sharot, T. (2011). The Optimism Bias: A Tour of the Irrationally Positive Brain. Pantheon Books. See also: Sharot, T. (2012). The optimism bias. TED2012. ted.com/talks/tali_sharot_the_optimism_bias
